Erastothenes lived in ancient Greece. He was born in 276 BC in Cyrene, Libya, and he died in 194 BC in Alexandria, Egypt. He was a famous mathematician, geographer, and astronomer. To see more information about Eratosthenes, click the picture below.

As a mathematician, Eratosthenes worked with prime numbers. He obtained the Sieve of Erastothenes, which is a method for finding all the prime numbers up to any number. To learn about the Sieve of Erastothenes, click the picture below.


















source: link


As a geographer, he measured the circumference of the Earth. He did so in Alexandria, Egypt by using angles and proportions. He measured the angle of the shadow made by a tower in Alexandria at noon on the longest day of the year, which he found to be 7.2 degrees. On same day at the same time in the city of Syene in Greece, the sun was directly overhead at 90 degrees, because it was shining straight down into a well. A circle is 360 degrees, so 7.2 degrees is 1/50 of a circle. He multiplied the distance between Syene and Alexandria by 50 to obtain a value of about 40,000 kilometers for the circumference of the Earth, which is remarkably close to the value of 40,075 kilometers obtained using today's technologies.
